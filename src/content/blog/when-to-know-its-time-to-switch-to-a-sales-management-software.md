Did you know that most sales reps are bogged down by administrative tasks, meetings, and various non-selling activities, leaving them with only about a third of their time to focus on actual selling?

Time is an expensive commodity in sales, and I am sure you don’t want your team spending more than half of their time on tasks unrelated to selling.

The good news is that times have changed for the better, and you no longer need to rely on outdated, tedious solutions like Excel or Spreadsheets to manage your sales. While Excel might be useful for companies with simpler workflows, in sales, where there are multiple steps in the workflow, Excel can do more harm than good by being time-consuming and prone to errors.

So, what are the modern solutions that can streamline your sales processes and help your team focus more on selling? 

But first, let’s explore the indicators that suggest it’s time to make a switch.

## Clear Signs It’s Time to Switch to Sales Management Software

In the dynamic world of sales, efficiency and accuracy are of the utmost importance. If you’re struggling with outdated processes and unreliable data, it might be time to consider upgrading to Sales Management Software (SMS). Here are two unmistakable signs that you need to make the switch:

### 1\. Your Team is spending more time on daily manual tasks than in the field.

A clear sign you should upgrade to Sales Management Software (SMS) is that daily manual tasks consume more of your team’s time than actual selling. Many businesses are still stuck in their old ways, manually handling tasks that could easily be automated with SMS, causing significant problems in the long run.

What are those daily manual tasks that are slowing you down?

#### **Manually Crunching Numbers on Quotes and Proposals**

This is a crucial task for any manufacturing rep firm, and even a small mistake can be costly. Manually handling quotes and proposals increases the risk of errors, such as incorrect pricing or product details. This can, in turn, lead to misunderstandings, client dissatisfaction, and potential loss of business. Without standardized templates, documents can vary in format and presentation, undermining your firm’s professional image and consistency.

#### **Slower Response Times**

Slow response times can lead to lost opportunities, as clients may turn to competitors who provide quicker turnaround. This inefficiency can significantly impact your firm’s ability to secure new customers and maintain a competitive advantage.

#### **Reduced Sales Productivity**

Unfortunately, sales representatives often find themselves bogged down by administrative tasks rather than focusing on what they do best: selling. As your business grows, the volume of quotes and proposals increases, and manual processes can create significant bottlenecks that hinder your growth and efficiency.

### 2\. Cleaning data is a weekly nuisance

Unclean data can deviate your sales and marketing efforts, impacting crucial tasks such as creating your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP), territory planning, segmentation, and sales prospecting. Additionally, this can also lead to problems with managing quotations, tracking follow-ups, and generating invoices.

A staggering 43% of sales and marketing teams report that inaccurate data is a significant challenge.  (source: Gartner)

Thus, Clean data is essential for reliable sales reports, identifying potential clients, ensuring timely follow-ups, and streamlining the invoicing process, ultimately driving your business forward.

#### Consequences of Messy Data

Misinformed Decision-Making

Inconsistent and Slower Sales Cycle

Unclean data creates roadblocks in your [sales cycle](https://www.yesware.com/blog/sales-cycle/), leading to poor lead management and prioritization. Sales reps may contact high-quality leads too late or not at all —slowing down the sales process and missing valuable opportunities.

## Sales Management Software Vs Spreadsheets

Spreadsheets might have been your go-to tool for tracking sales, but they can quickly become a liability, causing data errors and inefficiencies. Sales Management Software (SMS) is specifically designed to streamline your sales process, making it more efficient and effective. Here’s how an SMS can outperform spreadsheets:

![](/blog/blog-images/3-1024x576.png)

#### 1\. Enhanced Data Visibility

According to  79% of B2B sales professionals, having clear and accessible sales data is crucial for closing deals. However, spreadsheets can make data management messy. For example, if multiple team members enter data into different sheets, inconsistencies, and errors are bound to happen. An SMS, on the other hand, provides a centralized view of your sales data, allowing you to make informed decisions with ease. 

#### 2\. Superior Customer Service

Spreadsheets can make it difficult to manage and track customer interactions effectively. An SMS, on the other hand, offers robust customer relationship management features. For example, if a client follows up on a quote, an SMS ensures you can quickly access their previous interactions and respond promptly. This results in a positive experience and satisfaction. 

#### 3\. Streamlined Sales Cycle

Instead of manually creating each quote, an SMS can generate standardized quotes in just a few seconds, freeing up your sales team to focus on what they do best, i.e., sales.

An SMS automates key tasks such as generating quotes, managing follow-ups, and tracking leads. This automation speeds up your sales cycle, reduces human error, and helps you respond to client needs more swiftly. Manual processes in spreadsheets lead to delays and inefficiencies.

#### 4\. Efficient Task Management

Spreadsheets can be time-consuming, with hours spent on data entry and management. SMS automates daily tasks like scheduling follow-ups and updating lead statuses. This not only saves time but also ensures that no task falls through the cracks. Imagine your sales reps no longer having to enter data from paper forms manually—they can now focus on selling and building relationships instead.

#### 5\. Accurate Lead Management

Managing leads through spreadsheets can lead to lost opportunities and confusion.

However, an SMS  can automatically assign leads to sales reps based on predefined criteria, ensuring that high-quality leads are promptly addressed and each lead is nurtured appropriately.

In summary, switching to Sales Management Software enhances data visibility, improves customer service, streamlines your sales cycle, and automates routine tasks. This shift allows your sales team to operate more efficiently and effectively, ultimately driving business growth and success.

## Your Questions Answered

**1\. Is there a significant learning curve for our sales team to adapt to Sales Management Software?**

While any new software requires some level of learning, most Sales Management Software solutions are designed with user-friendliness in mind. They often include intuitive interfaces, tutorials, and customer support to help your sales team get up to speed quickly. Additionally, investing in initial training sessions can significantly reduce the learning curve.

**2\. Can Sales Management Software be customized to fit our unique sales process?**

Absolutely. [Sales Management Software](https://orgzit.com/#) solutions typically offer high levels of customization. You can tailor workflows, dashboards, and reporting features to align with your specific sales processes and business requirements. This flexibility helps in maximizing the software’s effectiveness for your team.

**3\. Can Sales Management Software scale with our business growth?**

Yes, one of the key benefits of Sales Management Software is its scalability. Whether you’re a small firm with a handful of sales reps or a large organization with a vast sales team, these solutions can grow with your business, accommodating increasing data volumes and expanding functionalities as needed.

**4\. What security measures are in place to protect our sensitive sales data?**

Sales Management Software providers prioritize data security and typically implement advanced security measures, such as encryption, two-factor authentication, and regular security audits. To safeguard sensitive information, it’s essential to choose a solution with strong data protection protocols.

**5\. How do we measure the ROI of implementing Sales Management Software?**

To measure the ROI, track key performance indicators (KPIs) such as sales productivity, deal close rates, response times, and customer satisfaction before and after implementation. Many Sales Management Software solutions also offer built-in reporting tools to help you monitor these metrics and evaluate the software’s impact on your business.
