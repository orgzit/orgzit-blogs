## Understanding Common CRM Database Issues

Dirty data can cost you more than sales; it can permanently damage your relationship with your customers. According to [IBM](https://hbr.org/2016/09/bad-data-costs-the-u-s-3-trillion-per-year), bad data costs U.S. companies three trillion dollars per year. Let’s take a look at the most common issue in this regard. 

### 1\. Data Duplication

If you do data entry and maintenance manually, chances are high that, without proper controls, you might end up with duplicate entries for the same client or order.

For instance, consider that John Smith, a client, is listed twice in your CRM database with slightly different spellings (“John Smith” and “Jon Smith”) Or a large order from one of your customers is recorded twice in your system, perhaps due to manual entry errors or system glitches.

Now, this duplication leads to misentries. Possibly partial entries in both records lead to confusion about which contact details and orders are accurate.

People might not know which information is correct. So, they might end up using the wrong details to contact or deal with the customer. This leads to confusion and mistakes in how you handle their orders or requests.

Think it through, If your reports and data analysis are based on duplicated information, they might not give you the right picture.

Fixing duplicate data takes time and effort. You have to find the duplicates, figure out which one is correct, and then merge them together. It’s like cleaning up a messy room before you can use it properly.

---

💡 Pro Tip: If you use Excel, use conditional formatting to highlight potential duplicates without having to scan through the data manually.

---

Apply a data duplication plug-in to your CRM database. Use unique identifiers, such as matching names and addresses.

### 2\. Data deletion

Data deletion is the removal of obsolete or superfluous information from a database. It happens for various reasons, including clients suspending operations or canceling orders abruptly.

Inactive client data occupies space within the database, potentially leading to unnecessary bloat. This can increase storage costs and slow down database performance, especially during queries and maintenance tasks. Over time, inactive client data may become outdated or irrelevant. This can degrade the overall quality of the database, making it more difficult to extract meaningful insights or perform accurate analysis.

Similarly, when a canceled order’s data remains in the system, it affects sales data and inventory estimates, which are critical to decision-making.

Inaccurate sales data can cause incorrect estimates, resulting in inventory imbalances such as overstocking or stockouts. Such differences have a direct impact on business operations.

On the other hand, regular data deletion helps to maintain a simplified database, allowing teams to focus on active possibilities and make sound decisions based on correct information.

### 3\. Incomplete or Inaccurate Data

Incomplete or inaccurate data in a database can lead to significant challenges and negative outcomes for businesses.

When client records lack essential details like contact information or purchase history, it becomes difficult to personalize interactions and anticipate client needs.

Without a complete picture of the client, reps struggle to provide tailored solutions and effectively engage with their customer base.

This leads to **lead leakage**, **missed sales opportunities, and reduced customer satisfaction.**

Similarly, sometimes a database shows ample inventory of a particular product, but the information is outdated or incorrect.

Relying on such inaccurate data can result in situations like stockouts or overstocking. Stockouts lead to lost sales opportunities, as customers are unable to purchase the product they need. On the other hand, overstocking ties up capital in excess inventory, leading to increased carrying costs.

It can also damage relationships with clients and suppliers, as delays or discrepancies in fulfilling orders may impact trust and satisfaction levels.

### 4\. Poor Data Quality

Poor quality data, like inconsistent customer records and incomplete sales data, can reduce efficiencies and impair decision-making.

If your customer database has **misspelled names** and **outdated contact details,** it can lead to, miscommunications, customer frustration, and bad branding.

You need accurate sales data to know how well you’re doing, spot trends, strategize, and plan for the future. Without it, you’re basically flying blind, which can really hurt your business in the long run.

So, accurate data is not just important, it’s essential. It helps you make smart decisions, keep your customers happy, and stay ahead of the competition.

### 10 Practical Methods to Keep Your CRM Database Clean and Up-to-Date

#### 1\. Regular Data Audits

One of the easiest yet most efficient ways to keep your data clean is through regular data audits.

You can conduct routine audits of your database to identify –

-   inconsistencies,
-   errors, and
-   outdated information.

For example, you can implement a system of regularly reviewing customer records to ensure accuracy and completeness. This will ensure that your database remains reliable and actionable.

---

💡 Schedule a “Data Cleanup Day” on the first Friday of every month.

---

**How to perform data audits**

By following these steps, you can effectively identify and address inconsistencies, errors, and outdated information in your database, ensuring its reliability and actionability for your business needs.

-   Identify parameters like correct formatting, up-to-date information, and absence of errors.
-   Use automated tools or manual audits, depending on the size and complexity of your database.
-   Cross-reference data with reliable sources to ensure accuracy.
-   Correct inconsistencies by updating records, removing duplicates, or reaching out to customers for verification.
-   Document findings to maintain transparency and accountability.

#### 2\. Standardize Data Entry

Another simple and quick way is to implement standardized formats and guidelines for data entry to maintain uniformity and consistency.

For instance, to establish clear formats for addresses, phone numbers, product names, and other data fields, **use data validation features in spreadsheet software or database** management systems to set specific criteria for data entry.

**Conditional formatting** can visually highlight entries that do not meet specified criteria, such as invalid phone numbers or long product names.

**Format cells in spreadsheet** software like Microsoft Excel to display data in a consistent manner, such as converting phone numbers to text.

Identify **key data fields** within the database or spreadsheet, such as designating specific columns for addresses, phone numbers, and product names.

**Use freeze panes** to keep header rows or columns visible while working with large datasets.

**Specify document formatting guidelines** and provide training to users on how to format addresses, phone numbers, and product names.

These options ensure consistent data formation and structure, making it easier to manage and analyze effectively.

#### 3\. Deduplication Tools

Technology is truly man’s best friend.

To avoid data duplication, you can always rely on deduplication tools to identify and merge duplicate records.

For manufacturers’ reps, this could mean consolidating multiple entries for the same client or order, ensuring that your CRM database remains clutter-free and accurate.

**How to deduplicate data**

**Identifying duplicate records** can be done using deduplication tools or by running queries based on criteria like customer name, email address, or order number.

**Merging the records** into a single record involves selecting one “master” record to retain and merge relevant information.

**Resolving conflicts** involves determining which information is accurate or prioritizing certain fields over others.

**Updating related** records: ensures data consistency across the database.

**Validating changes**, such as conducting post-de-duplication checks or audits, ensures the database remains accurate and functional.

#### 4\. Data Validation Rules:

You can set up validation rules to enforce data accuracy at the point of entry.

Let me make it simpler for you.

Setting up data validation rules implies creating a standard for your customers while filling in the required information.

---

💡 Validation rules are like those little helpers behind the scenes checking what your customers type. If they miss out on it, a message might pop up saying, “Please enter a valid email address or phone number.”

---

Data validation rules are quite crucial in data management systems to ensure data accuracy, reliability, and consistency. They enforce specific standards for data entry, preventing errors and inconsistencies.

In the domain of business, accurate data is essential for informed decisions and reliable analyses. Validation rules prevent the entry of invalid or incorrect data, ensuring the data remains trustworthy over time.

**How to Set Up Validation Rules**

To ensure data accuracy and validity;

-   Identify specific requirements, such as data type, format, range, or character presence.

-   Define validation criteria based on these requirements, which can be simple or complex.

-   Implement these rules within the data entry interface or management system, using built-in features or customizing them.

-   Provide meaningful feedback to users when data fails to meet the criteria, explaining the error and how to correct it.

-   Test and refine the rules based on user feedback and real-world usage scenarios to ensure they effectively catch errors and enforce data standards.

Data validation rules also streamline the data entry process by providing real-time feedback and reducing errors. Consistent data is crucial for accurate reports, meaningful analyses, and data exchange between systems.

Some typical examples of data validation rules are:

**email validation** (requires email addresses to have an “@” symbol and valid domain name)

**numerical validation** (specifies acceptable numeric values for a field)

**date validation** (date validation ensures valid dates are in a specified format)

**required fields** (required fields mandate specific fields to be filled out)

**format validation** (format validation enforces specific data entry formats.)

####  5. Update Contact Information

Regularly keep updating the contact information of your customers to ensure that you can reach customers and prospects effectively.

Maintaining accurate contact information is crucial for effective communication with customers and prospects.

It enhances customer satisfaction and improves business efficiency by ensuring timely delivery of **important updates, marketing messages**, and customer support, reducing wasted time and resources.

**How to Update Contact Information Regularly:**

-   Regular reviews of customer contact information can identify outdated or incorrect details, which can be done manually or automated through CRM software.

-   Customers can be prompted to update their information during interactions, such as email reminders or phone calls.

-   Offering multiple channels for updating information, such as online forms, email, phone, or in-person visits.

-   Leveraging technology can streamline the process. For example, provide pre-filled forms with existing customer data for easy review and editing.

---

**💡 Pro Tip:** Some of the best ways to get your hands on updated contact information include:

_Email verification_: requires customers to verify their email addresses when updating their contact information online.

_Annual check-ins:_ conducted to review and update contact details through personalized outreach or automated email campaigns.

_Subscription Renewal_: Customers must verify their contact information before renewing subscriptions or memberships to ensure up-to-date communication channels.

_Event registrations_: require attendees to verify their contact details for timely updates and communications.

#### 6\. Segmentation and Tagging

Segmenting your database enables _targeted marketing campaigns, personalized communication, and lead leakage prevention_.

By delivering relevant messages to each segment, tagging records based on criteria enhances the customer experience and develops stronger relationships. This also prevents potential opportunities from falling through the cracks.

To segment your database, define criteria like **industry, location, purchase history, demographics, behavior, interests, or engagement level**.

Collect data from various sources and segment the database into distinct groups. Tag records based on additional criteria and use automation tools or CRM software to streamline the process and ensure consistency across segments.

For example, you can create segments for customers based on their

-   industries like healthcare, technology, finance, retail, etc.

-   based on their geographic location, such as country, region, or city.

-   purchase history, such as frequent buyers, first-time purchasers, or customers who have made large-ticket purchases.

-   level of engagement with your brand, such as active users, dormant users, or high-value customers.

This allows you to deliver localized marketing campaigns and promotions.

#### 8\. Remove Inactive Records

You must periodically remove inactive records or disengaged contacts from your database.

Regularly removing inactive records from your database improves database efficiency, reduces clutter, and enhances data quality.

This ensures marketing efforts are targeted towards active contacts, generating value for your business and enhancing the effectiveness of marketing campaigns and customer interactions.

For reps, this might involve archiving clients who haven’t placed orders in a certain timeframe. This keeps your database focused on active and engaged contacts.

**How to Identify and Remove Inactive Records**

-   To identify inactive records, define criteria such as lack of activity, no purchases, or minimal engagement.
-   Use database or CRM software to identify records that meet these criteria.
-   Review and confirm the list to ensure accuracy. Decide whether to archive or remove inactive records, either for future reference or to streamline efficiency.
-   Use automation tools to automate the process and set up regular intervals for review and purging.

---

💡 **Pro Tip:** You can archive clients who haven’t placed orders within the past six months or archive contacts who haven’t attended any events or webinars in the past year. Remove those who haven’t opened marketing emails or clicked on links in the past year, and remove those who haven’t visited the website.

#### 9\. Train Your Team

Comprehensive training on CRM database management improves data quality, efficiency, and decision-making by ensuring team members understand the importance of maintaining clean, up-to-date data.

This training streamlines processes, reduces errors, and empowers them to contribute to data-driven decision-making within the organization.

Offer guidance on data entry, validation, and maintenance to ensure that everyone contributes to keeping the database clear.

In order to do that, assess team knowledge and skill levels, identify areas needing additional training, and develop comprehensive training materials.

Conduct hands-on workshops, tailor training sessions to specific roles, and schedule regular updates to ensure everyone remains proficient in their tasks.

Tailor training sessions to specific roles, such as sales representatives or marketing specialists, to ensure they understand the importance of database maintenance.

#### 10\. Backup Data Regularly

Regular backups are crucial for preventing data loss, ensuring business continuity, and preventing lead leakage.

The good thing about automated backups is that they enable quick data recovery, restoring databases to their previous state with minimal downtime.

This helps maintain the integrity and confidentiality of customer data, even in the event of data loss or corruption.

**Steps to Regularly Backup Your Database:**

-   Select the right backup solution for your database system and organization, such as [cloud-based or on-premises services.](/blog/cloud-based-vs-on-premise-manufacturer-rep-software-which-is-right-for-your-business/) 

-   Schedule regular backups based on your company’s operations and data update frequency.

-   Reduce the need for manual intervention by automating backup procedures.

-   Testing and restoring procedures on a regular basis ensures their reliability.

-   Monitor the backup progress to verify it is completed successfully and set up alerts for any issues.
