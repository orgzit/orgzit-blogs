## What is a Bidding process?

Starting from the basics, the bidding process selects a vendor for subcontracting a project or purchasing products and services required. Bid records contain the specifications of the project or details of the products and services to be purchased.

A typical bidding process consists of the following steps:

1.  Developing a bid outline by the project manager with product specifications and costs of the project
2.  The manager issues the bid
3.  The bid undergoes an approval process
4.  After approval, the bid is distributed to a group of subcontractors or vendors
5.  The vendor or subcontractors submit the cost of the project
6.  The manager manages the bids responses and analyzes them
7.  The manager awards the bid to the vendor or subcontractor that meets the requirements
8.  A standard contract is then created by using bid details and bid responses

## Challenges of the Manual Bidding Process

While managing bid records, selecting the best bids that meet the project requirements, and preparing a contract are essential tasks in the bidding process, it is equally crucial to follow up on the bids. This ensures timely communication and effective collaboration with subcontractors, vendors, and other stakeholders involved. Demonstrating competence and professionalism throughout the bidding process establishes a strong reputation and builds trust with your business partners. Effective bid process management is vital in enhancing your B2B relationships and can greatly contribute to your overall success in the bidding process.

The manual bidding process for B2B sales team can present several challenges, including:

1.  Time-Consuming: Manual bidding involves significant paperwork, manual data entry, and communication.

2.  Human Error: The reliance on manual data entry increases the risk of human error. Mistakes in recording and transferring bid information can lead to inaccuracies, misunderstandings, and potential losses.

3.  Lack of Centralized Information: Maintaining a centralized bid information repository can be challenging with manual bidding. This makes accessing and tracking bid documents, communication records, and important details throughout the process difficult.

4.  Limited Visibility: Manual bidding processes may lack transparency and real-time visibility into the status of bids. This can make monitoring progress, identifying bottlenecks, and ensuring timely follow-up challenging.

5.  Inefficient Communication: Manual bidding often involves phone calls, emails, and physical documentation exchanges. This can result in communication gaps, miscommunications, and difficulties keeping all stakeholders informed and aligned.

6.  Difficulty in Bid Comparison: Manual bidding makes it laborious to compare bids side by side, especially when dealing with multiple vendors and complex pricing structures. This can hinder the effective evaluation and selection of the most competitive bids.
